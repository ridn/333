Key

While animation can certainly be fun, attractive, and often even helpful to users, its important to know its limits. Users enjoy animations only as long as they don't impede on getting something done. It is well known that end users can be impatient, especially when using the web or applications. Should a page take an obnoxious amount of time to animate before its loaded and usable, productivity decreases. Given that the average time spent on a particular webpage is under a minute, using animations liberally severely cuts into that time. Furthermore, an animation can help draw attention to how things are intended to be used or make actions feel more natural. Incorrect use just for aesthetics can further complicate a user interface and alienate a user. It looks pretty, but I can't figure out how to do anything. That aside, properly employed animation can be helpful, feel natural, and add fluidity to an interface. Not to mention that lacking any sort of animation would also alienate users from lack of excitement. Most importantly they can be really fun to watch and interact with!
